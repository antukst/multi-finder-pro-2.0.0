# Text Multi-Finder: Verification Patterns (Reference)

This document provides a vast list of common survey attention checks and verification patterns. You can copy these into the "Verification Patterns" tab in the extension to automatically highlight them.

## 1. Instructional Manipulation Checks (IMC)

_Respondents are told to select a specific answer or ignore the regular question._

- "Select 'Strongly Disagree' for this item to confirm you are reading."
- "Please ignore the question below and select the third option."
- "To show you're paying attention, please select 'Other' and type 'Verified' in the box."
- "Choose 'Very Unlikely' to verify your attention."
- "Select the second and fourth checkboxes only."
- "Click 'Proceed' only if you have read the terms above."
- "Scroll to the bottom and click the hidden blue dot."
- "Ignore the scale and select 'Neutral' for this row."

## 2. Factual Attention Checks

_Simple questions with one objectively correct answer._

- "What color is the sky on a clear day?"
- "How many days are in a week?"
- "What is 2 + 3?"
- "Which of the following is a dairy product?"
- "What is the capital of France?"
- "Which of these is NOT a fruit: Apple, Banana, Elephant?"
- "Select the number that comes after three."
- "Which animal is known for barking?"

## 3. Bogus Items & Nonsensical Statements

_ABSURD statements that an attentive human would disagree with._

- "I have never used a mobile phone in my life."
- "I can run faster than a cheetah."
- "I live on the surface of the moon."
- "I have not slept in the last 10 years."
- "I work 400 hours per week."
- "Selection: Rock, Bunny Rabbit, Human, Magic Carpet."
- "Cereal Brand: Marshmallow Brownie Crisps (Fake)."

## 4. Consistency Checks & Red Herrings

_Identifying rushers or inconsistent responders._

- "What is your favorite flavor of ice cream? (Option: Soccer)"
- "I always vote in elections." (Check for inverse later)
- "I never vote in elections."
